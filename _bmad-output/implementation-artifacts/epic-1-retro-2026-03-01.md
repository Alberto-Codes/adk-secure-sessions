## What Went Well

### We shipped
The entire point of Epic 1 was to get from brownfield repo to live on PyPI. v1.0.0 is published, installable, and announced via Discussion #74. The team anchored on this as the defining success.

### Code review matured into a genuine quality gate
By mid-epic, the review process was catching real issues before release — unused `sqlalchemy` dependency (Story 1.9), missing `dependabot.yml` (Story 1.10), inaccurate PBKDF2 attribution (Story 1.8), wrong benchmark `addopts` (Story 1.5). The system worked.

### Test infrastructure paid dividends from Story 1.1 onward
The conftest deduplication, benchmark marker registration, and fixture organization in Story 1.1 created a foundation that every subsequent story built on. Coverage grew steadily from 119 tests to 167 tests without fixture spaghetti.

### Knowledge cascade via Previous Story Intelligence
Story files accumulated lessons from prior stories, preventing repeated mistakes. By Story 1.6, each new story already knew what pitfalls to avoid.

### Clean trunk migration
Moving from `develop` to `main` (Story 1.10) with branch protection rules completed without losing commits or breaking CI.

### Full CI/CD pipeline with trusted publishing
Publish pipeline (Story 1.11) with 5-job chain, pip-audit, smoke tests, and OIDC trusted publishing — production-grade from day one.

## What Didn't Go Well

### Brownfield confusion created noise
Inherited artifacts (`sqlalchemy` dependency, `bump-minor-pre-major` dead config, stale warning filters) caused confusion throughout the epic. The team was doing archaeological cleanup alongside new construction.

### Inaccurate claims in documentation
Code review caught factual inaccuracies in 5+ stories — "all cryptographic operations use PBKDF2" (wrong attribution), "3 runtime dependencies" (4 listed), `BACKEND_FERNET` string vs. byte assertions. Claims outran verification.

### Blast radius analysis missed peripheral files
Story 1.10 missed `dependabot.yml` entirely — a HIGH finding. The blast radius checks focused on source and test files but overlooked CI configs and tooling.

### Benchmark targets were aspirational
Story 1.5 targeted 20% encryption overhead but measured 2.4-3.5x locally. Fernet plus async wrapping is inherently heavier on small payloads. Required CI soft-warning workaround.

### First-attempt CI failures
Publish pipeline smoke-test (Story 1.11) failed on first run due to missing `uv venv` in the CI environment. Classic "works on my machine" — resolved quickly but delayed the pipeline.

---

## Key Insights

1. **The review process IS the safety net** — brownfield mess gets caught, not shipped. The system worked even when individual stories had gaps.
2. **Epic 2 is the natural cleanup pass** — documentation and accuracy gaps inherited from brownfield are addressed by the Documentation & Compliance Credibility epic.
3. **Having a reference project (docvet) in our portfolio** dramatically reduces setup risk for docs-heavy work in Epic 2.
4. **Tech debt is known and catalogued, not hidden** — address opportunistically when relevant epics touch the affected areas, not in dedicated debt sprints.
